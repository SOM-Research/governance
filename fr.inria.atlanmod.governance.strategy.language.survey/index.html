On the definition of a language for governance in OSS

Survey

Motivation

We performed a recent survey to understand the problems people face when trying to contribute to open source projects (OSS). The results shown that the level of difficulty to contribute increases as the contribution task requires more implementation effort (e.g., submitting a patch) or communication between the people involved (e.g., knowing the status of your contribution).

To alleviate this problem we think of defining a tool which facilitates the definition of governance rules in software projects. The objective is to provide contributors a clear way to understand how the project is developed.

The language

The language allows defining strategies to follow in the governance of a project. These strategies can be adapted to the very own nature of each particular project.

Strategies to make decisions

  • LeaderDriven: One or more people make the decision arbitrarily
  • Democratic: Decisions are made by voting. The range considers those that are present or not in the moment of voting. The ratio of majority can also be set
  • Phased-based: Decisions are made in a phased fashion

Collaboration types

Main artefacts around which decisions are made in a development project

  • Bug
  • Enhancement
  • Comment (can become a bug or an enhancement)

Planification

To avoid an endless decision processes, deadlines have to be met. They can be based on:

  • Time (hours, days, weeks, etc.)
  • Who voted
  • A special condition

The survey

Case 1

The following code defines a strategy where the project leader is making all the decisions

strategy1: LeaderDriven {
	applied to 	All               // All collaboration types
	when 		TaskAcceptance
	deadline	1 week
	who		ProjectLeader
}

And will be verbalized as

All the bugs, enhancements and comments will be accepted or rejected in one week by the leader group.

Case 2

The following code defines a strategy where bugs are accepted if more than a half of the vote results agreed

strategy2: Majority {
	applied to	Bug
	when		TaskAceptance
	deadline	1 month
	who		Anyone	  // Anyone can vote
	range		Present  // Participants present in the moment of voting
	minVotes	2
}

And will be verbalized as

Bugs will be accepted or rejected in 1 month according to a majority voting process with at least two votes. Anyone can participate and only the total number of votes will be considered to calculate the ratio.
