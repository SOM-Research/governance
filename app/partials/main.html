What is this?

When developing software systems, we have to face a long-life process where we cope with a huge number of development tasks (e.g., implementing new features or fixing bugs). To success, it becomes crucial to effectively and precisely prioritize these tasks.

For that, we normally define and apply what is normally referred as governance rules, in plain English, a set of instructions telling us how we can create tasks and how decisions regarding their acceptance/rejection are going to be made.

Despite their importance, in practice, we discovered governance rules are hardly ever explicitly defined, especially in the context of Open Source Systems (OSS).

This tool will allow you to make explicit the governance rules in your projects

How can I define my governance rules?

We have created a simple language to define governance. You can download the tool here.

To make things easier we have also developed a form-based generator that creates instances of our DSL and also their English translation. Just play with the form in this webpage to start obtaining your rules!

We envision the use of a kind of governance.md files where anyone can easily understand how our project is governed. For this, our DSL is only the starting point, but we believe it may help to achieve it.
